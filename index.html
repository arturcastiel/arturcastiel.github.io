Artur Castiel, PhD

Researcher - Mechanical Engineer - Programmer

Hello there! I am Dr. Artur Castiel Reis de Souza, a passionate researcher with a strong interest in computational fluid dynamics and the development of simulators for hydrogen storage, carbon capture storage, enhanced geothermal systems, and various subsurface porous media applications. I am currently working as a post-doctoral researcher at TU-Delft in the Netherlands, where I am a proud member of the ADMIRE group. My research pursuits primarily focus on linear and non-linear non-orthodox flux approximations, as well as techniques to generalize the Multiscale Finite Volume family of methods to function effectively with unstructured grids. I love solving complex problems and contributing to the advancement of engineering.

Impress

The IMPRESS is an intuitive Python pre-processor devised to make multiscale and multilevel simulation straightforward.

The data structure that describes the mesh is organized hierarchically making it simple to access any information.

On IMPRESS information is encapsulated, so the mesh objects were designed to allow componentization of the simulation code.

On multiscale and multilevel simulation, the coarse volumes are components of the high-resolution mesh-object. Coarse volume information is accessed just like any other mesh-object.

The IMPRESS project is one of the product of my first year as a PhD student. It was designed by me to aid me in my goal to create a 3d multiscale multilevel simulator suitable for unstructured grids. It was later adopted by other members of PADMEC (high performance computing on computational mechanics) group that have stepped in to help me improve this framework.

DARSim

DARSim is a user-friendly MATLAB-based open-source 3D reservoir simulator developed at TU-Delft designed to be a prototype space for researchers and students. It provides a flexible environment where users can experiment with various multiphase flow simulations in natural underground formations. I am currently the main developers of the DARSim code.
